Ron Ross

co-founder FORBIX

Ron Ross began his career on Wall Street at Merrill Lynch where at the age of 23 he obtained his General Securities and Commodity Broker licenses. He later formed a non-bank dealer where his firm was a market maker in the Interbank Foreign Exchange Market with weekly transactional volume in excess of $1 Billion.

Ron has over 25 years of experience in trading equity and interest rate derivative products. In 2001, he transitioned his knowledge of finance into real estate and mortgage banking. His real estate experience spans that of new development, acquiring and repositioning of distressed properties, and asset management.

In 2011, Ron co-founded Forbix Financial, an FHA Title II lender. He has a Bachelor of Science in Finance from Pepperdine University.

Emil Khodorkovsky

co-founder FORBIX

Emil Khodorkovsky immigrated to the United States at the age of 10 to Harrisburg, PA. He attended Penn State University and then Cal State Northridge for Finance and Real Estate. In 2001 he began his career in an entry level position at a mortgage company. He quickly advanced and became a partner at the firm. During that time, he began investing in real estate through private trust deeds, acquisition of performing and non-performing debt, as well as real estate. In 2011 he started Forbix and established a commercial mortgage bank, a direct lending platform for private debt, and an acquisition and development arm, all under one roof.

He has vast knowledge in the areas of real estate finance, asset management, construction and has acted as a court appointed receiver for real estate properties in California. Clients value his advice in real estate related matters including 1031 exchanges, multi-family developments, CMBS financing, and FHA Apartment and Construction financing.
